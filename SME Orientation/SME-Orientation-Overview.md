# SME Orientation: Welcome to Course Development at Unity DE

Welcome to the course development process at Unity Environmental University's Distance Education program! This orientation is designed to help you understand the philosophy, structure, roles, and logistics of developing an online course with the Learning Experience Design team.

## About This Orientation

This orientation is structured for **self-paced learning** and can be completed in approximately **2 hours**. Each section is designed to be independent, so you can read in any order that makes sense for you.

**Recommended Order:**
1. [Course Design at Unity](#course-design-at-unity)
2. [Your Role and Authority](#your-role-and-authority)
3. [Course Components and Alignment](#course-components-and-alignment)
4. [Technologies and Tools](#technologies-and-tools)
5. [Development Timeline](#development-timeline)
6. [Coming Prepared to Your First Meeting](#coming-prepared-to-your-first-meeting)

## Course Design at Unity

> **Duration:** 15-20 minutes

Learn about the philosophy behind course development at Unity DE, including backwards design, the iterative nature of course maintenance, and credit hour requirements.

**Key Concepts:**
- Outcomes-focused course design
- Course maintenance and iterative development
- Credit hour requirements for undergraduate and graduate courses

**Key Files:**
- [Course Design at Unity](Course%20Design/Course-Design.md)
- [Course Project Philosophy](Course%20Design/Course-Project-Philosophy.md)
- [Equity and Inclusivity in Course Design](Course%20Design/Equity-and-Inclusivity-Considerations-for-LXD-Designing-Courses.md)

**Reflection Questions:**
- What does "backwards design" mean to you?
- How do you currently think about measuring student learning?
- What real-world deliverables exist in your field?

## Your Role and Authority

> **Duration:** 10-15 minutes

Understand your role as a Subject Matter Expert, the Learning Experience Designer's role, and the Dean's authority in course development. Clarity on roles prevents confusion and keeps development moving smoothly.

**Key Concepts:**
- SME vs. LXD responsibilities
- LXD has development authority
- Dean has ultimate authority and veto power
- Collaborative decision-making in practice

**Key Files:**
- [Role, Scope, and Authority](Roles/Role-Scope-and-Authority.md)
- [Intellectual Property](Roles/Intellectual-Property.md)

**Reflection Questions:**
- What questions do you have about your role?
- What decisions do you expect to make? Which ones do you expect your LXD to make?
- What would you do if you disagreed with an LXD decision?

## Course Components and Alignment

> **Duration:** 25-35 minutes

Understand how all course elements—outcomes, graded activities, learning materials, and media—work together to support student learning.

**Key Concepts:**
- The alignment chain from program outcomes to daily activities
- Four types of graded activities: Course Projects, Assignments, Discussions, and Quizzes
- Instructor expectations for quality and rigor
- Media and learning materials standards

**Key Files:**
- [Components Overview](Course%20Components/Components-Overview.md) (comprehensive)
- [Assignments and Discussions](Course%20Components/Assignments-and-Discussions.md)
- [Course Projects](Course%20Design/Course-Project-Philosophy.md)

**Reflection Questions:**
- How do the assignments you currently create align to learning outcomes?
- What activities in your field are most authentic? How could they become assessments?
- How would you scaffold a complex skill across an 8-week course?

## Development Timeline

> **Duration:** 15-20 minutes

Understand the development process from start to finish, including major milestones, approval gates, weekly workflows, and time commitment expectations.

**Key Concepts:**
- Timeline varies by course credit (8-16 weeks)
- Four major milestones with Dean approval gates
- Weekly rhythm and meeting expectations
- Time commitment: 8-18 hours per week
- What to do if you fall behind

**Key Files:**
- [Timeline and Milestones](Timeline/Timeline-and-Milestones.md)
- [Quality Assurance Review](Timeline/QA-Review.md)

**Reflection Questions:**
- What is your realistic time availability per week?
- Which part of the process feels most uncertain to you?
- What would help you stay on track?

## Coming Prepared to Your First Meeting

> **Duration:** 20-30 minutes

Before you meet with your Learning Designer, there are specific ways to prepare that will make the process much smoother and faster.

**Key Concepts:**
- Understanding the course documentation you'll receive
- Bloom's Taxonomy and higher-order thinking
- Brainstorming your Course Project
- Gathering resources and materials

**Key Files:**
- [What to Expect When SMEing a Unity DE Course](Coming%20Prepared%20to%20first%20meeting/What-to-Expect-when-SMEing-a-Unity-DE-Course.md)
- [Course Project Pre-Approval Template](Coming%20Prepared%20to%20first%20meeting/Course-Project-Pre-Approval-Template.md)

**Recommended Action Items:**
1. Read your course description and outcomes carefully
2. Review the Bloom's Taxonomy guide
3. Complete the Course Project Pre-Approval Template with 1-3 project ideas
4. Gather 5-10 potential learning materials (articles, videos, etc.)
5. Jot down questions for your first meeting

**Bloom's Resources to Review:**
- [Bloom's Taxonomy Teacher Planning Kit](https://www.cebm.net/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/Blooms-Taxonomy-Teacher-Planning-Kit.pdf)
- [Webb's Depth of Knowledge](https://www.aps.edu/sapr/documents/resources/Webbs_DOK_Guide.pdf)

## Key Contacts

**Before Course Development Starts:**
- Your **Learning Designer** - Project management, instructional design, day-to-day collaboration

## Quick Reference: The Development Process at a Glance

```
START
   ↓
Kickoff → Course Outcomes Approved → Course Project Approved
   ↓              ↓                       ↓
(Week 1-2)    (Week 3-4)             (Week 5-8)
   ↓              ↓                       ↓
Draft course activities and materials
   ↓              ↓
(Week 9-14)
   ↓
LXD builds course in Canvas
   ↓
(Week 14-15)
   ↓
SME QA Review (1 week)
   ↓
Corrections made
   ↓
(Week 15-16)
   ↓
Dean Final Approval
   ↓
COMPLETE → Course goes to Blueprint
```
